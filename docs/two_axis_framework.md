# 2-Axis 4-Quadrant Framework for GPT Impact Assessment

## Overview

This framework evaluates the societal impact of General Purpose Technologies (GPTs) along two orthogonal axes, producing four distinct quadrants of impact.

## Axes

### Y-Axis: Quality of Life Impact
Measured by the 6-dimension QoL composite score (-5 to +5).
Emphasizes subjective, emotional, aesthetic, and cultural dimensions over material indicators.

Positive direction: enrichment of inner life, sensory experience, emotional depth, cultural sensitivity
Negative direction: emotional flattening, aesthetic homogenization, loss of meaning

### X-Axis: Macro-Economic Contribution
Measured by economic macro score (-5 to +5).
Standard economic indicators: GDP contribution, productivity gains, market creation, employment effects.

Positive direction: economic growth, productivity, market creation
Negative direction: economic disruption, job displacement, market destruction

## Four Quadrants

```
              Quality of Life +
                    |
        II          |         I
   QoL+/Econ-      |    QoL+/Econ+
   "Hidden          |    "Win-Win"
    Enrichment"     |
                    |
 ───────────────────┼──────────────────  Economic +
                    |
        III         |         IV
   QoL-/Econ-      |    QoL-/Econ+
   "Lose-Lose"     |    "Growth at
                    |     Cost"
                    |
              Quality of Life -
```

### Quadrant I: Win-Win (QoL+, Econ+)
Technology enhances both life quality and economic growth.
- Example: Printing press → literacy → skilled workforce AND cultural enrichment
- Example: Telephone → business efficiency AND personal relationship deepening

### Quadrant II: Hidden Enrichment (QoL+, Econ-)
Technology enhances life quality without measurable economic contribution.
**This is the primary target quadrant for route identification.**
- Example: Automobile → road trips (leisure, no direct GDP contribution but emotional richment)
- Example: Phonograph repurposed from dictation → music appreciation
- Example: Telephone → "chatting" culture (no economic value but relational enrichment)

### Quadrant III: Lose-Lose (QoL-, Econ-)
Technology degrades both life quality and economic outcomes.
- Example: Early automobile pollution + accident costs
- Example: Information overload reducing both productivity and well-being

### Quadrant IV: Growth at Cost (QoL-, Econ+)
Technology drives economic growth at the expense of life quality.
- Example: Automobile → suburban sprawl (construction GDP) + social isolation
- Example: Factory automation → productivity + worker alienation

## Route Analysis

### Key Question
How do GPTs travel through these quadrants over time? Is there a typical trajectory?

### Hypothesis: The Enrichment Detour
Many GPTs follow a pattern:
1. **Initial deployment**: Quadrant IV (economic purpose, quality-neutral or negative)
2. **User appropriation**: Shift toward Quadrant I or II as users discover non-economic uses
3. **Cultural maturation**: Stabilization in Quadrant I/II as cultural practices solidify

This "enrichment detour" is often non-linear and driven by grassroots innovation rather than inventor intent.

### Non-Linear Path Types
- **Serendipity**: Unintended beneficial use (phonograph → music)
- **Repurpose**: Deliberate redirection by users (telephone → social chat)
- **Grassroots**: Bottom-up cultural adoption (automobile → dating culture)
- **Resistance**: Quality-of-life improvement through resistance to technology (Slow Food movement)
- **Appropriation**: Marginalized groups finding empowerment (bicycle → women's mobility)
- **Creative misuse**: Artistic/creative repurposing (turntablism)
- **Democratization**: Elite technology becoming universally accessible

## Application to Robotics

The framework will be applied to project potential robotics routes into Quadrant I/II:
- Which robotics applications are likely to enhance QoL?
- Which historical GPT patterns suggest non-obvious QoL-enhancing routes for robotics?
- What conditions enable the "enrichment detour" from Quadrant IV to Quadrant I/II?
