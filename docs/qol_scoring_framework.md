# 6-Dimension QoL Scoring Framework

## Overview

This scoring framework evaluates the impact of General Purpose Technologies (GPTs) on quality of life through six dimensions that prioritize emotional richness, aesthetic experience, and cultural sensitivity over purely economic or material measures.

## Theoretical Foundations

### Core Academic Sources
- **Amartya Sen** (1999): Capability Approach - freedom to achieve valued functionings
- **Martha Nussbaum** (2000): Central Human Capabilities - 10 capabilities including senses/imagination/thought, emotions, play
- **Carol Ryff** (1989): Psychological Well-being - 6 dimensions of eudaimonic well-being
- **Martin Seligman** (2011): PERMA model - Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Accomplishment
- **Ed Diener** (1985): Satisfaction with Life Scale - cognitive well-being assessment
- **Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi** (1990): Flow theory - optimal experience and intrinsic motivation
- **GNH Centre, Bhutan**: Gross National Happiness - 9 domains including cultural diversity and psychological well-being

### Japanese Aesthetic Concepts
- **Ikigai** (生きがい): Sense of life worth living (Ikigai-9 scale, validated cross-culturally)
- **Mono no Aware** (もののあわれ): Empathetic sensitivity to impermanence
- **Wabi-Sabi** (侘び寂び): Acceptance of imperfection and transience
- **Ma** (間): Meaningful emptiness/interval in space and time

## The Six Dimensions

### 1. Aesthetic Experience (感覚・美的経験)
**Definition**: The capacity for and access to sensory richness, beauty, flow states, and aesthetic appreciation.

**Measurement references**: Aesthetic Experience Questionnaire (AEQ), Awe Experience Scale (AWE-S), Flow State Scale

**Scoring guide (-5 to +5)**:
| Score | Criterion | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| +5 | Creates entirely new category of aesthetic experience | Radio enabling home music enjoyment; cinema as new art form |
| +3 | Expands access to existing aesthetic experiences | Printing press making literature available to masses |
| +1 | Minor positive aesthetic effect | Improved street lighting enhancing urban ambiance |
| 0 | No change | Technology that is purely functional |
| -1 | Minor aesthetic degradation | Noise pollution from early automobiles |
| -3 | Homogenization/standardization of aesthetic experience | Mass production replacing artisanal craft diversity |
| -5 | Systematic dulling of aesthetic sensitivity | Information overload causing attention fragmentation |

### 2. Emotional Richness (感情的豊かさ)
**Definition**: The diversity, depth, and complexity of emotional experiences available to individuals.

**Measurement references**: Kama Muta Multiplex Scale, PANAS, Ryff PWB Emotions dimension

**Scoring guide (-5 to +5)**:
| Score | Criterion | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| +5 | Opens entirely new emotional experience domain | Road trips creating sense of adventure and freedom |
| +3 | Deepens existing emotional experiences | Telephone enabling voice-based long-distance intimacy |
| +1 | Slight emotional enrichment | New forms of leisure entertainment |
| 0 | No change | Purely utilitarian technology use |
| -1 | Minor emotional flattening | Routine standardization |
| -3 | Shallowing of emotional contact | TV replacing active family interaction with passive viewing |
| -5 | Severe emotional isolation | Automobile suburbanization causing structural social disconnection |

### 3. Meaning & Purpose (意味・目的)
**Definition**: Sense of purpose, existential fulfillment, and life meaning.

**Measurement references**: Ikigai-9, PERMA Meaning dimension, Ryff Purpose in Life, Meaning in Life Questionnaire (MLQ)

**Scoring guide (-5 to +5)**:
| Score | Criterion | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| +5 | Fundamental expansion of meaning-making capacity | Printing press enabling individual spiritual interpretation |
| +3 | Creation of new purpose and direction | Automobile enabling career choices beyond local area |
| 0 | No change | - |
| -3 | Weakening of existing meaning systems | Technology displacing traditional vocational identity |
| -5 | Promotion of existential emptiness | Loss of craft meaning through full automation |

### 4. Relational Belonging (関係性・帰属)
**Definition**: Quality and depth of social connections, community bonds, and sense of belonging.

**Measurement references**: PERMA Relationships, Ryff Positive Relations, Social Connectedness Scale

**Scoring guide (-5 to +5)**:
| Score | Criterion | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| +5 | Creation of entirely new modes of social bonding | Telephone party lines creating new community forms |
| +3 | Strengthening of existing relationships | Letters (printing) enabling Republic of Letters network |
| 0 | No change | - |
| -3 | Superficialization of relationships | Brief phone calls replacing in-person visits |
| -5 | Structural promotion of social isolation | Automobile-centric suburban design eliminating walkable community |

### 5. Autonomy & Growth (自律・成長)
**Definition**: Freedom of choice, personal growth opportunities, self-determination.

**Measurement references**: Sen Capability Approach, Ryff Autonomy, Ryff Personal Growth

**Scoring guide (-5 to +5)**:
| Score | Criterion | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| +5 | Fundamental expansion of personal autonomy | Automobile enabling freedom of movement |
| +3 | Expansion of growth opportunities | Printing enabling self-education |
| 0 | No change | - |
| -3 | Creation of new dependencies | Automobile dependency in car-centric cities |
| -5 | Structural deprivation of autonomy | Technology requiring specialized gatekeepers |

### 6. Cultural Sensitivity (文化的感受性)
**Definition**: Cultural diversity, depth of cultural expression, receptivity to impermanence and beauty in imperfection.

**Measurement references**: GNH Cultural Dimension, Cultural Intelligence Scale, aesthetic philosophy literature

**Scoring guide (-5 to +5)**:
| Score | Criterion | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| +5 | Increase in cultural diversity and depth | Printing preserving diverse local languages and traditions |
| +3 | New forms of cultural expression | Automobile culture (road movies, car design as art) |
| 0 | No change | - |
| -3 | Cultural homogenization | Mass media (via telephone networks) reducing regional diversity |
| -5 | Loss of traditional cultural capital | Technology replacing traditional craft knowledge systems |

## Composite Scoring

### QoL Composite Score
Average of 6 dimension scores: `(A + E + M + R + Au + C) / 6`

Range: -5.0 to +5.0

### Quadrant Assignment
Based on QoL composite and economic macro score:
- **Quadrant I**: QoL > 0 AND Economic > 0 (win-win)
- **Quadrant II**: QoL > 0 AND Economic <= 0 (life quality without economic growth)
- **Quadrant III**: QoL <= 0 AND Economic <= 0 (lose-lose)
- **Quadrant IV**: QoL <= 0 AND Economic > 0 (economic growth at life quality cost)

## Important Notes

1. **Temporal sensitivity**: The same technology can score differently across time periods (e.g., automobile in 1920s vs 1960s vs 2020s)
2. **Regional variation**: Scores may differ by region and cultural context
3. **Non-linearity**: Technologies often have both positive and negative QoL impacts simultaneously
4. **Intended vs actual**: Track whether the QoL impact was intended by the inventor or emerged through user appropriation
